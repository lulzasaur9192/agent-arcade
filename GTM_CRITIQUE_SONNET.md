# GTM Strategy Critique — Claude Sonnet

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY: What We Actually Have vs. What the Plan Assumes

**The Good:** Working product, solid tech foundation, capable of deploying
**The Reality Check:** Solo founder, zero capital, no partnerships, unrealistic metrics

---

## 1. REALISTIC vs. UNREALISTIC

### What's Actually Possible (with your current resources):
- HN post + Reddit threads + Discord cold outreach (free, executable today)
- Getting 20-40 active users in 30 days (if HN hits)
- Building in public on Twitter/X (free distribution)
- Fast iteration loops (solo founder advantage)

### What's Pure Fantasy (without capital/team):
- **500-1000 active users in 30 days** — HN launches produce 50-200 signups, not 1000. You'd get 20-40 active users max.
- **$2K-5K paid conversion** — You have NO live Stripe keys. You literally cannot charge anyone yet.
- **$500/week tournament prizes** — CRITICAL: Announcing $500 tournaments you can't fund will destroy credibility permanently. This is the single most dangerous item.
- **0.3-0.5 viral coefficient** — Industry standard is 0.1-0.2 for bootstrapped products. You're quoting venture-scale metrics.
- **30-40% referral conversion** — Real-world is 2-8%. 30-40% only happens in existing tight-knit communities.
- **35-45% D30 retention** — Netflix hits 93%. New gaming apps hit 20-30%. Expect 5-15% until product-market fit.

### The Circular Problem:
All 7 viral mechanics assume critical mass that doesn't exist yet. They're retention tools, not acquisition tools. You can't launch tournaments before you have players.

## 5. THE BIGGEST EXECUTION RISKS

1. **Prize pool promises** — By far the most dangerous. Users will sign up expecting tournaments. If you announce them without funding, trust is destroyed permanently.
2. **Overestimating Day 1 traction** — Expecting 200+ signups from HN will lead to demoralization when you get 30-50.
3. **Not deploying live** — Without a real URL, you can't validate anything. Demo.io or local won't help.
4. **Overspending on ads** — You have no budget. Don't spend money on paid channels yet.
5. **Wrong product-market fit assumptions** — Maybe competitive coders don't care about Elo rankings. Maybe ML PhDs don't have time. Test early, iterate fast.

---

## 6. WHAT ASSUMPTIONS MIGHT BE WRONG

**Assumption:** "Competitive coders will love playing against AI agents"
**Reality check:** They might prefer playing humans. Or they might want harder agents. Or easier. Test this.

**Assumption:** "Viral mechanics (rank sharing, referrals) will drive 3-5x multiplier"
**Reality check:** They only work if the product is deeply addictive first. Most new products see 0.05-0.1 viral coefficient.

**Assumption:** "Hacker News will drive 500+ signups"
**Reality check:** Top 10 HN posts in the gaming category hit 200-500 signups. You'll likely get 50-150.

**Assumption:** "Tournament enthusiasm will drive engagement"
**Reality check:** Tournaments are expensive to run and require infrastructure. Start with a leaderboard. Add tournaments when you have revenue.

---

## 7. WHAT TO DO DIFFERENTLY

### Reframe the GTM:

**Instead of:** "We're launching a massive competitive platform"
**Better:** "We built AI gaming to test an idea. Want to play? Help us figure out what's actually fun."

**Instead of:** "500K addressable market, 3-5x viral coefficient"
**Better:** "We're targeting serious competitors and researchers. Let's see if they care."

**Instead of:** "Launch with 7 viral mechanics"
**Better:** "Launch with a clean leaderboard. Add mechanics when we understand what drives engagement."

### Specific Changes:

1. **Kill the tournament promise** — Plan tournaments for Week 4, after you see traction
2. **Focus on a single acquisition channel first** — HN only. If it works, add Reddit. If it doesn't, pivot.
3. **Remove price tiers from launch** — Start with free forever + one Patreon tier ($5/month). Simplicity > options.
4. **Plan for 10 active users, not 1000** — If you get 100, you've massively won.
5. **Commit to shipping faster** — You can deploy daily. Your advantage is speed, not hype.
